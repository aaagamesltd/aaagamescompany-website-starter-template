Dev Blog: The Sound Design Behind Our Scary Didgeridoo

The didgeridoo is more than just an ancient instrument—it’s a storytelling tool that has shaped our vision for All Hallows. Traditionally carved from hollowed-out eucalyptus branches by Indigenous Australians, each didgeridoo has a unique timbre, tone, and personality. These natural variations inspired the layered soundscapes and eerie audio cues within the game.

Our team spent countless hours experimenting with different tones, mouthpiece shapes, and resonance techniques. This process not only informed the game’s audio-driven puzzles but also influenced the core design ethos: a blend of mystery, rhythm, and cultural authenticity. In fact, elements of the didgeridoo’s spiral patterns subtly appear in our logo, symbolizing both sound waves and the journey players take through haunted musical realms.

By embracing the didgeridoo’s organic imperfections, we were able to craft a gameplay experience where sound is both a guide and a challenge. Players will encounter zones where subtle tonal shifts reveal hidden paths, and rhythmic pulses unlock secrets. It’s an homage to the instrument itself—a bridge between music, storytelling, and interactive horror.

We hope this blog post gives you a glimpse into the audio design philosophy that drives All Hallows. The didgeridoo is just one of the many tools we’ve used to immerse players in a world where sound tells the story and guides every step.
